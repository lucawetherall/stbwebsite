Each year St Barnabas is one of the hosts — or sends volunteers to other venues — for the
**Ealing Churches Winter Night Shelter**. The project provides emergency, dormitory-style
accommodation for up to fourteen guests: people who are sleeping on the streets, at immediate
risk of rough sleeping locally, or among the ‘hidden homeless’.

The shelter runs from late November to the end of March. Each night a different church hosts
guests, offering a hot meal, a bed, breakfast and — just as importantly — companionship. On
average a guest stays around four weeks before moving on to more permanent housing.

> ‘Truly I tell you, whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of
> mine, you did for me.’ — Matthew 25:40

You can find out more about the project at the
[Ealing Churches Winter Night Shelter](http://www.ecwns.org.uk/). To volunteer or donate,
please [contact the parish office](/contact-us).
